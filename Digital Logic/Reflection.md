Digital Logic introduced me to the fundamental concepts that form the foundation of modern computer systems. Throughout the course, I learned about Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational circuits, sequential circuits, and circuit simplification techniques such as Karnaugh Maps. These concepts helped me understand how digital devices process and store information at the hardware level. Practical exercises and circuit design tasks enhanced my analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities while demonstrating how theoretical concepts are applied in real systems. Overall, the subject provided a strong foundation for understanding computer architecture and hardware design.
